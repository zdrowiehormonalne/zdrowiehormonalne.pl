When should you consider a medical consultation?

Do you recognize any of these symptoms? They can have many causes — testosterone deficiency is one of them. A medical consultation will help clarify what is happening.

Chronic fatigue and low energy

You wake up tired, even after a full night's sleep.

Brain fog

Hard to focus, get started, or follow through on things.

Sleep and recovery problems

Sleep doesn't feel restful, your body takes longer to recover.

Decreased libido

Less interest in sex, or something that used to come naturally has changed.

Weight gain despite effort

Your body composition is changing even though your lifestyle hasn't.

Abnormal hormonal test results

You have results but don't know what to make of them.

How does the diagnostic process work?

The diagnostic process includes several stages that allow for a thorough assessment of the patient's health.

01

Medical consultation

Detailed medical interview and symptom analysis

02

Laboratory tests

Ordering appropriate hormonal tests

03

Results discussion

Interpretation of results and explanation of next steps

04

Further management

Establishing a treatment plan if medical indications exist

Treatment is implemented only when medical indications exist.

Regular follow-up visits and timely completion of ordered tests are an essential part of safe therapy. The patient plays an active role in this process — the physician provides care, while responsibility for attending scheduled check-ups lies with the patient.

Testosterone deficiency treatment

Testosterone replacement therapy (TRT) is one of the treatment methods used in patients with confirmed testosterone deficiency.

Each case requires individual medical assessment, appropriate diagnostics, and regular monitoring during treatment.

The decision to implement treatment is made solely by a physician after completing full diagnostics and analyzing test results.

Telemedycyna w endokrynologii — jak wygląda zdalna konsultacja hormonalna?

Konsultacja zdalna stała się standardem dla wielu specjalności medycznych. W przypadku zaburzeń hormonalnych teleporada sprawdza się wyjątkowo dobrze — dowiedz się, jak wygląda i jak się do niej przygotować.

6 min czytania

SHBG — czym jest i jak wpływa na poziom aktywnego testosteronu?

Mężczyźni często przychodzą na konsultację z wynikiem testosteronu "w normie", ale z wyraźnymi objawami niedoboru. Częstą przyczyną tej rozbieżności jest właśnie SHBG — białko, które decyduje, ile testosteronu jest faktycznie dostępne dla organizmu.
